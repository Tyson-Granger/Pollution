AQI

(AQI) Air Quality Index is used by government to inform the public about how polluted the air is or it can be in the future. Public health risks increase as the AQI rises. Different countries have their own air quality indices, corresponding to different national air quality standards.


Air Pollution

Air pollution is a mixture of particles and gases in the air. Car emissions, chemicals from factories, dust, pollen are some examples. Ozone, a gas, is a major part of air pollution in cities. Pollution formed by ozone is called smog. Some air pollutants are harmful and dengerous.
